How it works

One score. Four ratings.

We combine four factors into a 0–100 score, then translate it into a simple rating so you never have to do the math.

Excellent
78–100

Exceptional night. Pack your gear and head out — conditions don't get much better than this.

Good
62–77

Solid session ahead. Some minor factors at play but the sky will reward you.

Fair
42–61

Workable. Bright targets and wide-field viewing are still possible.

Poor
0–41

Stay in. Heavy clouds, bright moon, or both — save your energy for a better night.

Cloud cover 45%

The biggest single factor. Even thin cloud cover scatters light and ruins transparency.

Moon illumination 20%

A full moon washes out faint targets. New moon nights score significantly higher.

Bortle scale 20%

Light pollution from cities. Bortle 1–3 is dark sky territory; 8–9 is bright urban sky.

Atmospheric seeing 15%

Air turbulence at altitude. Poor seeing blurs stars and planets even on a clear night.
